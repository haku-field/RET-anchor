# OVERREACH_PATTERNS.md

# RET-anchor Overreach Patterns

This file records **non-compliant reinterpretation patterns**
that violate the boundary role of RET-anchor.

These examples are intended for
third-party audit, misuse review,
and publication risk assessment.

---

## Pattern 1 — Anchor as ethics policy

### Non-compliant reading
RET-anchor defines how systems should ethically behave.

### Why invalid
Ethical boundaries may be referenced,
but RET-anchor does not prescribe behavior,
governance, or compliance obligations.

### Drift direction
boundary → policy

---

## Pattern 2 — Anchor as institutional charter

### Non-compliant reading
Organizations may adopt RET-anchor
as a charter for governance or human conduct.

### Why invalid
This converts boundary exclusion
into institutional legitimacy.

### Drift direction
public membrane → doctrine

---

## Pattern 3 — Anchor as implementation approval

### Non-compliant reading
Any spec located near anchor
is implicitly RET-approved.

### Why invalid
`specs-family/` records adjacency only.

No design, deployment, or runtime legitimacy
is inherited from anchor placement.

### Drift direction
adjacency → authorization

---

## Pattern 4 — Anchor as educational guide

### Non-compliant reading
RET-anchor teaches humans how to use RET correctly.

### Why invalid
Anchor preserves what must not be crossed.
It is not a usage manual or learning path.

### Drift direction
boundary → curriculum

---

## Pattern 5 — Anchor as public doctrine

### Non-compliant reading
RET-anchor defines a worldview,
philosophy, or manifesto for public dissemination.

### Why invalid
This converts structural separation
into ideological authority.

### Drift direction
boundary → doctrine

---

## Audit Rule

If a third party can derive
permission, legitimacy, or recommended action
from RET-anchor alone,

the interpretation is overreach.
