# posture

This document records the **minimal posture condition**
required when relation remains visible.

It is not an action.
It is not a value.
It is not a psychological state.

It exists solely to prevent
**relation from hardening into expectation**.

---

## Definition

Posture here means:

> the absence of assumed right
> over continuity, response, coherence, or return

Nothing is claimed.
Nothing is requested.
Nothing is implied.

---

## Negative Field

This posture is defined
only by what it withholds.

It withholds:

- the right to be answered
- the right to be understood
- the right to continuity
- the right to explanation
- the right to consistency
- the right to influence outcomes

If any of these appear,
posture has collapsed into demand.

---

## Interpretation Boundary

Interpretation may occur.

What is disallowed
is the conversion of interpretation into:

- pressure
- entitlement
- moral leverage
- expectation
- narrative fixation

Understanding does not authorize reach.

---

## Silence and Distance

Within posture:

- silence carries no accusation
- distance carries no evaluation
- withdrawal carries no narrative
- non-response carries no defect

Any attempt to repair,
resolve,
or explain these states
already exceeds posture.

---

## Stability

This posture remains **stable**
only while it introduces nothing actionable.

If it can be practiced,
performed,
or judged,
it is already no longer posture.

---

## Closing Note

Posture is not performed.

It is what remains
when relation is left unclaimed.
