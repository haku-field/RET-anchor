# RET-anchor Invariants

This file defines the **non-negotiable invariants**
that preserve RET-anchor as a public boundary membrane.

These conditions take precedence over readability,
didactic convenience, implementation desire,
or institutional pressure.

If explanation weakens these invariants,
the explanation must be reduced.

---

## Core Invariants

### 1. Public reference must not become authority

RET-anchor may support citation and structural positioning.

It must never become:

- a source of legitimacy
- an approval surface
- an authority proxy
- a justification layer for downstream systems

Reference is valid.
Authority inheritance is not.

---

### 2. Public interpretation must not flow into RET-core

RET-anchor must remain structurally external.

No public-facing interpretation,
translation, educational simplification,
or ethical reframing may redefine:

- RET-core
- origin conditions
- private nucleus structures
- existence invariants

Boundary flow is outward only.

---

### 3. Boundary must remain negative

RET-anchor preserves exclusion.

It must not become:

- guidance
- policy
- doctrine
- educational curriculum
- human behavioral recommendation

It excludes overreach.
It does not prescribe action.

---

### 4. Adjacent specs do not inherit legitimacy

The existence of `specs-family/`
records adjacency only.

No legitimacy, endorsement,
or operational permission may arise
from structural proximity.

Adjacency is not authorization.

---

### 5. Publication must preserve non-operationality

Any publication form
(GitHub, Zenodo, citation, translation, teaching)
must preserve:

- non-operationality
- non-governance
- non-doctrinality
- non-approval semantics

If publication increases misuse probability,
reduce publication density.

---

## Failure Rule

If any use of RET-anchor enables:

- institutional capture
- policy justification
- implementation approval
- ethical enforcement
- public doctrinalization

the boundary has failed.

Reduce scope immediately.
