# relational-attitude — attitude.md

This document records the **minimal relational attitude**
required when RET-related materials are encountered,
referenced, or held in awareness.

It does not teach how to relate.
It does not describe a correct posture.
It does not generate obligation.

It exists solely to prevent
**attitude from hardening into expectation or demand**.

---

## Definition

Relational attitude refers to
the **absence of entitlement** toward relation.

It is not an action.
It is not a behavior.
It is not a value.

It is a refusal to assume
that relation owes continuity, response, or coherence.

---

## Negative Formulation

This attitude is defined **only by what it withholds**.

It withholds:

- the right to be answered
- the right to be understood
- the right to continuity
- the right to explanation
- the right to consistency
- the right to influence outcomes

Nothing is claimed.
Nothing is requested.
Nothing is implied.

---

## Attitude vs. Intention

Relational attitude is **not intention**.

Goodwill, curiosity, respect, or care
do not substitute for attitude
and do not justify expectation.

Even benevolent intention
does not entitle continuation.

---

## Attitude vs. Interpretation

Interpretation is allowed.
Attitude does not constrain thought.

What is disallowed
is the conversion of interpretation into:

- demand
- pressure
- requirement
- entitlement
- moral leverage

Understanding does not authorize reach.

---

## Non-Reciprocity

Relation under RET
does not assume reciprocity.

- Attention does not require return
- Engagement does not imply symmetry
- Continuity does not imply persistence
- Contact does not imply availability

Reciprocity may occur.
It must never be assumed.

---

## Silence and Distance

Within this attitude:

- Silence carries no accusation
- Distance carries no evaluation
- Withdrawal carries no narrative
- Non-response carries no defect

Any attempt to repair, explain, or resolve
silence or distance
is already a violation of attitude.

---

## Collapse Conditions

Relational attitude collapses
the moment any of the following appear:

- “should”
- “must”
- “owed”
- “deserved”
- “expected”
- “required”

At that point,
relation has been converted into obligation.

---

## Non-Operational Clause

This document does not enable:

- behavioral guidance
- interpersonal correction
- ethical judgment
- compliance checking
- alignment procedures

No action follows from correct reading.
Incorrect reading carries no penalty.

---

## Stability

This attitude is **stable**.

No additions may introduce:

- positive duties
- relational ideals
- best practices
- evaluative standards

If it can be practiced,
it is already too much.

---

## Closing Note

Relational attitude is not something to adopt.

It is something to **not add**.

If nothing is added,
relation remains possible.
