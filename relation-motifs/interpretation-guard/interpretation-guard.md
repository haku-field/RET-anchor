# interpretation-guard — relation motif

This document records **interpretation-guard** as a relation motif.

Interpretation-guard defines a condition
in which interpretation itself is **constrained, withheld, or refused**
to prevent relation from being converted into meaning, authority, or action.

It is not correction.
It is not silence.
It is not skepticism.

It exists to prevent
**interpretation from becoming intervention**.

---

## Position

This protocol belongs to:

```text
RET-anchor

    └ interpretation-guard
       └ interpretation-guard.md
```

It is **external**, **non-operational**, and **non-evaluative**.

It does not derive authority
from RET-core,
outer-contour,
specs-family,
or any adjacent motif.

---

## Definition

Interpretation-guard is the condition in which:

- meaning is not finalized
- intent is not inferred
- motive is not attributed
- coherence is not demanded
- explanation is not imposed

Interpretation may occur.
It must not **settle**.

Guarding interpretation does not deny thought.
It denies **closure**.

---

## What Interpretation-Guard Withholds

Interpretation-guard withholds:

- the right to conclude intent
- the right to assign motive
- the right to define state
- the right to explain behavior
- the right to stabilize meaning

Nothing is corrected.
Nothing is resolved.

---

## Non-Conversion Clauses

Interpretation-guard must not be converted into:

- analysis or diagnosis
- clarification requests
- narrative framing
- meaning-making authority
- epistemic closure

Understanding does not authorize fixing.

---

## Relation to Other Protocols

Interpretation-guard may coexist with:

- presence (dwelling)
- distance
- silence
- response

It does not override them.
It constrains **how they are read**.

Interpretation-guard does not replace
canonical-term-guard.
It does not protect vocabulary.
It protects **relation from semantic capture**.

---

## Misreadings to Avoid

The following interpretations are **invalid**:

- “Interpretation-guard blocks thinking”
- “Interpretation-guard enforces silence”
- “Interpretation-guard denies meaning”
- “Interpretation-guard is relativism”

It does none of these.

It only blocks **conversion**.

---

## Non-Operational Clause

This document does not enable:

- interpretive authority
- epistemic hierarchy
- therapeutic framing
- behavioral correction
- meaning enforcement

No action follows from guarded interpretation.
No failure follows from ambiguity.

---

## Stability

Interpretation-guard is **stable**.

Clarifications may reduce language,
but will not introduce:

- explanatory function
- evaluative outcome
- decision authority
- operational effect

If interpretation starts to act,
it has ceased to be guarded.

---

## Closing Note

Interpretation is not a right.

It is a tendency.

Interpretation-guard exists
to keep that tendency
from becoming power.
