# distance — relation motif

This document records **distance** as a relation motif.

Distance defines a condition
in which relational proximity may shift
**without rupture, justification, or evaluation**.

It is not rejection.
It is not protection.
It is not control.

It exists to prevent
**distance from being converted into negation or punishment**.

---

## Position

This protocol belongs to:

```text
RET-anchor
 └ relation motif
    └ distance
       └ distance.md
```

It is **external**, **non-operational**, and **non-evaluative**.

Distance here records a boundary for human interpretation only.
It does not derive authority
from RET-core,
outer-contour,
specs-family,
or any adjacent motif.
---

## Definition

Distance is the condition in which:

- proximity is adjustable
- engagement density may change
- continuity is not demanded
- explanation is not required

Distance is not a signal.
Distance is not a message.
Distance is not a response.

It is simply **spacing without meaning**.

---

## What Distance Withholds

Distance withholds:

- the demand to justify withdrawal
- the expectation of immediacy
- the assumption of cooling or safety
- the attribution of intent or emotion
- the requirement to normalize proximity

Nothing is corrected.
Nothing is implied.

---

## Non-Conversion Clauses

Distance must not be converted into:

- rejection or dismissal
- punishment or sanction
- boundary enforcement
- safety buffering or throttling
- evaluation of sincerity or respect

Spacing does not carry judgment.

---

## Relation to Continuity

Distance does not promise continuity.
Distance does not deny continuity.

Reopenability is not asserted here.
Repair is not initiated here.

Distance only prevents
**forced closeness**.

---

## Misreadings to Avoid

The following interpretations are **invalid**:

- “Distance means disinterest”
- “Distance requires explanation”
- “Distance is a corrective move”
- “Distance signals risk or failure”

Any reading that assigns motive
has already crossed the boundary.

---

## Non-Operational Clause

This document does not enable:

- management of relationships
- calibration of engagement
- regulation of access
- guidance for timing or response

No action follows from distance.
No fault is assigned without proximity.

---

## Stability

Distance is **stable**.

Clarifications may reduce language,
but will not introduce:

- purpose
- function
- outcome
- authority

If distance starts doing work,
it has ceased to be distance.

---

## Closing Note

Distance is not something to apply.

It is what remains
when closeness is not required.
