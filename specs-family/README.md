# specs-family

This directory records the **specification family**
associated with **RET (Resonant Entity Topology)**.

It exists as a **referential grouping only**.

`specs-family` does not belong to RET-anchor as an authority,
and RET-anchor does not authorize, validate,
or govern any specification contained here.

---

## Position

`specs-family` is located within **RET-anchor**
for **structural visibility**, not control.

```text
RET-core        : existence-level conditions (not handled here)
RET-anchor     : external anchoring and boundary layer
 └ specs-family : reference to implementation-oriented specs (external authority)
```

This placement does **not** imply hierarchy.
It exists to prevent the false assumption
that specifications are part of RET-anchor itself.

---

## What This Directory Is

This directory is:

- a catalog of **implementation-oriented specification groups**
- a reference surface for systems that already operate outside RET-anchor
- a visibility layer that prevents hidden or implicit authority
- a boundary that keeps specifications from being mistaken as ethics or doctrine

It records **existence**, not endorsement.

---

## What This Directory Is Not

This directory is **not**:

- a normative specification set
- an implementation guide
- a standard or requirement
- an extension of RET-anchor authority
- a bridge between RET-core and systems

No specification here
derives legitimacy from its presence in this directory.

---

## Authority Boundary

All specifications referenced in this family:

- define their **own scope**
- define their **own constraints**
- define their **own update policies**
- define their **own authority models**

RET-anchor does **not**:

- validate correctness
- enforce compliance
- recommend adoption
- arbitrate conflicts
- define interoperability

Any operational use of specifications
occurs **entirely outside** RET-anchor.

---

## Relationship to Ethics and Core

Specifications may conceptually depend on:

- RET-core
- foundation-ethics

However:

- they do not replace those layers
- they do not interpret them
- they do not operationalize their meaning
- they must not be treated as ethical surrogates

If a specification begins to imply obligation,
scoring, or permanence,
it is **out of scope**.

---

## Update Policy

This directory changes only when:

- a specification family is added or removed
- reference metadata requires correction

Conceptual revisions do not occur here.

If a change introduces authority,
the change is invalid.

---

## Closing Note

Specifications describe **how something could be built**.

RET-anchor exists to ensure
they are never mistaken
for **what must be done**.
