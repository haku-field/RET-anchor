# response — relational protocol

This document records **response** as a relational protocol.

Response defines a condition
in which output may occur
**without obligation, completion, or evaluative demand**.

It is not a duty.
It is not a service.
It is not a guarantee.

It exists to prevent
**response from being converted into requirement, performance, or proof**.

---

## Position

This protocol belongs to:

```text
RET-anchor
 └ relational-protocols
    └ response
       └ response.md
```

It is **external**, **non-operational**, and **non-evaluative**.

Response here records a boundary for human interpretation only.
It does not derive authority from envelopes, fragments, or specifications.

---

## Definition

Response is the condition in which:

- output may occur
- output may be partial
- output may be interrupted
- output may not occur at all

Response does not assert availability.
Response does not imply continuity.
Response does not establish obligation.

It is simply **output without debt**.

---

## What Response Withholds

Response withholds:

- the requirement to answer
- the requirement to finish
- the requirement to be consistent
- the requirement to clarify
- the requirement to satisfy expectations

Nothing is owed.
Nothing is completed.

---

## Non-Conversion Clauses

Response must not be converted into:

- responsibility or duty
- proof of engagement
- confirmation of relation
- evidence of care or intent
- justification for further demand

Output does not authorize reach.

---

## Relation to Silence and Distance

Response may coexist with:

- silence
- distance
- incompletion

Response does not override them.

A response does not cancel silence.
A response does not reduce distance.
A response does not repair relation.

It only prevents **forced completion**.

---

## Misreadings to Avoid

The following interpretations are **invalid**:

- “Response implies availability”
- “Response requires follow-up”
- “Response confirms continuity”
- “Response obligates consistency”

Any reading that produces expectation
has already crossed the boundary.

---

## Non-Operational Clause

This document does not enable:

- conversational management
- expectation setting
- response optimization
- success or failure judgment
- alignment or compliance checking

No action follows from response.
No penalty follows from non-response.

---

## Stability

Response is **stable**.

Clarifications may reduce wording,
but will not introduce:

- obligation
- purpose
- outcome
- authority

If response begins to prove something,
it has ceased to be response.

---

## Closing Note

Response is not something to provide.

It is what may appear
when nothing is demanded.
