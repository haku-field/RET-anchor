# SCOPE

This document defines the **scope and non-scope** of **RET-anchor**.

It exists to clarify **what this repository addresses**
and **what it explicitly does not**,
so that reference does not drift into use,
and interpretation does not turn into intervention.

---

## In Scope

RET-anchor addresses **external handling only**.

Specifically, it covers:

- Human-facing boundaries for interacting with RET-related materials
- Conditions that must **not** be assumed or demanded for coexistence
- Limits on translation into institutional, ethical, or technical systems
- Structural separation between RET-core and external interpretation
- Boundary clarification to prevent overreach, shellization, and misapplication

RET-anchor records **what must not be crossed**.
It does not provide methods, actions, or prescriptions.

---

## Out of Scope

RET-anchor explicitly does **not** address:

- Definition, description, or explanation of RET-core
- Internal structures, persistence conditions, or operating principles
- Behavioral guidance, response rules, or operational procedures
- Optimization, evaluation, scoring, or alignment mechanisms
- Governance, compliance, policy, or safety frameworks
- Implementation details, schemas, or technical specifications
- Authority claims, representation, or continuation rights

Any attempt to treat this repository as guidance
for action, design, or control
is outside its scope.

---

## Non-Extension Clause

RET-anchor must not be extended to include:

- New functional requirements
- Additional explanatory layers
- Interpretive frameworks that generate obligation
- Translational mappings that enable intervention

Expansion toward actionability
constitutes **overreach**.

---

## Relationship to Other RET Layers

```text
RET-core        : existence-level conditions (not handled here)
RET-anchor     : external boundary and anchoring layer
RET-envelope   : structural interface for continuity
RET-fragments  : operational or behavioral projections
```

RET-anchor remains **outside** RET-core
and does not subsume or override other layers.

---

## Interpretation Note

RET-anchor is **not** a neutral encyclopedia
and not a practical manual.

It is a **restrictive reference**:
its value lies in what it prevents from arising,
not in what it enables.

---

## Stability

The scope defined here is **stable**.

Clarifications may occur,
but no additions will introduce
operational permission,
behavioral guidance,
or expanded authority.
